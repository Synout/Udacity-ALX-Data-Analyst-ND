Part II - PISA2012 Student Scores in Maths, Science, Reading and Other Features Correlating To Them¶

by Mohamed E. Rahmani¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, a preview on the scores in reading, mathematics and science are visualized against gender, country, out of school activities, and age start learning.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consists of more than 400,000 entries and over 600 features of interest. I chose 27 of these features and focused on plausible values 1 for each subject: science, mathematics and reading. Focusing on the plausible values as student performance indicators, I cross examined them with gender of students, age they started learning, and wealth tendency.

Scores Distribution¶

The distribution of scores was normally distributed with no significant skewness. This means that the mean and median of the scores are equal, with averages of scores at: Maths 485.93, Science 484.69, Reading 479.67

Student Gender vs Scores¶

The differences in mathematics and science scores were minimal between males and females genders. On average, males scored higher on maths than females by 8.5 points, and higher by 4 points than females on science, while females scored significantly higher on reading test by 38.1 points.

Student Age Started Learning vs Scores¶

The most prominent effect on scores was the age the student atarted learning. In this feature, there was a clear inversely proportional relationship with the scores: the later the student begins learning in their lifetime, the lower the scores tend to be.

Student Age Started Learning vs Wealth¶

Now that we have seen a clear relation between age and score, I wanted to see what other features could relate to age start learning factor. In wealth tendency feature, there is a visible decline in wealth tendency with students who started at a later age of their lifetime. This concludes that students who begin learning at an earlier stage of their lifetime have better wealth (~ 0) tendency than those who started at a later stage (< 0).
